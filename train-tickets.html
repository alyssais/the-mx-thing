Train Operator Websites

Just the ticket

You can use any of these websites to book tickets for any National Rail journey in the UK. You aren't limited to the operator's site for their own trains, and be aware of third party websites because they often charge booking fees, while the websites run by actual train operators generally don't.

Doesn't ask for title at all

  • Chiltern Railways - chilternrailways.co.uk

Title is optional

  • Virgin Trains West Coast - virgintrains.co.uk
  • Virgin Trains East Coast - virgintrainseastcoast.com - mandatory but includes options "None", "Mx" and "Other", which prompts you to add your own.
  • Arriva Trains Wales - arrivatrainswales.co.uk
  • East Midlands Trains - eastmidlandstrains.co.uk
  • Scotrail - scotrail.co.uk
  • Grand Central - grandcentralrail.com
  • CrossCountry - crosscountrytrains.co.uk
  • Greater Anglia - greateranglia.co.uk

Title is mandatory but custom titles can be entered and/or "Mx" is an option

  • Great Western Railway (except Night Riviera) - gwr.com
  • TransPennine Express - tpexpress.co.uk - Bonus points from me for rebranding some trains to "TransPride Express" during Manchester Pride festival.
  • South Western Railway - southwesternrailway.com
  • Hull Trains - hulltrains.co.uk

Title is mandatory and only offer gendered titles

I will be contacting these companies soon.

  • Northern (Arriva Rail North) - northernrailway.co.uk - Old Northern Rail (Serco-Abellio) site had title as optional. Existing accounts have not had a title forced on them.
  • LondonMidland - londonmidland.com - Old ticket booking engine had title as optional. Existing accounts have not had a title forced on them it seems.
  • Southeastern - southeasternrailway.co.uk
  • Southern - londonmidland.com
  • Heathrow Express - heathrowexpress.com
  • Thameslink - thameslinkrailway.com
  • Great Northern - greatnorthernrail.com

For sleeper service tickets, see the dedicated page.

Merseyrail and Heathrow Connect have no ticket buying or other personal account system of their own on their websites.

Summary

Going through all these websites in quick succession shows that there's actually only about 4 different booking systems which are rebranded accordingly. Each have their own title options off-the-shelf, so perhaps one changing would prompt the others to follow suit. I will be contacting those listed above who only offer gendered titles to ask them what's up.
